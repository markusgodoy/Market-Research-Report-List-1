<p><p>The extruded food market is divided into two types: Hot Extrusion Food Processing and Cold Extrusion Food Processing. Hot Extrusion involves cooking and shaping the food mixture through high temperature and pressure, resulting in products like pasta and breakfast cereals. Cold Extrusion, on the other hand, utilizes lower temperatures and pressures, maintaining the nutritional value and creating products like protein bars and snacks. Both processes involve forcing the food mixture through a die to form a desired shape and texture, offering a wide range of convenient and flavorful food options to consumers.</p></p>
