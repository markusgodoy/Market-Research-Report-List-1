<p><p>Cable modems are devices used to access the internet by transmitting data over cable television lines. There are two market types for cable modems: wired and wireless. Wired cable modems connect to the internet through a physical cable, typically a coaxial cable, that is connected to the modem and the internet service provider's network. Wireless cable modems, on the other hand, utilize wireless technology, such as Wi-Fi, to transmit data wirelessly to devices like computers and smartphones. These two market types offer different options for consumers based on their connectivity needs and preferences.</p></p>
