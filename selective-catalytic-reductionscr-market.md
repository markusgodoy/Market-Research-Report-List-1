<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is a technology used to reduce nitrogen oxide emissions from diesel engines by converting them into nitrogen gas and water vapor. The process involves injecting a liquid reductant, such as urea, into the exhaust stream, where it reacts with the nitrogen oxides over a catalyst to form harmless gases.</p><p>The SCR market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.4% during the forecast period. The growth of the market is driven by increasing regulations on emissions from diesel engines, particularly in the automotive and transportation sectors. Countries around the world are adopting stringent emission standards to combat air pollution and reduce the impact of nitrogen oxides on public health and the environment.</p><p>The latest trends in the SCR market include the development of advanced catalyst materials and systems that offer higher efficiency and durability, as well as the integration of SCR technology into new vehicle models and industrial applications.
