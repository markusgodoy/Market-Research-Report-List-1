<p><p>Citation management software refers to tools that help researchers and scholars organize their references and citations. Cloud-based software operates on remote servers accessible via the internet, allowing users to store and access their data online. Web-based software, on the other hand, is accessed through a web browser without any need for installation. Both types offer similar functionalities, including organizing references, creating bibliographies, and inserting citations into documents. The main difference lies in the infrastructure: cloud-based software relies on remote servers, while web-based software is accessed directly through a browser.</p></p>
