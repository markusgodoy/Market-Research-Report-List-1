<p><p>Lower limb arterial stents are medical devices used to treat peripheral artery disease (PAD), a condition in which arteries in the lower extremities become narrowed or blocked. These stents are placed in the affected arteries to improve blood flow and alleviate symptoms such as pain, cramping, and difficulty walking.</p><p>The lower limb arterial stents market is expected to witness steady growth during the forecast period, with a projected CAGR of 4.40%. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the rising prevalence of PAD, especially among the aging population, is driving the demand for lower limb arterial stents. As the global population ages, the incidence of PAD and associated complications such as critical limb ischemia (CLI) is increasing.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements in stent design and material composition are also contributing to market growth. Manufacturers are developing innovative stent designs with better flexibility, improved radial strength, and reduced risk of restenosis. These advancements lead to improved clinical outcomes and patient satisfaction.</p><p>Additionally, the increasing adoption of minimally invasive procedures for lower limb arterial interventions is fueling the demand for stents. Endovascular procedures require smaller incisions, reduce the risk of complications, and offer quicker recovery times compared to traditional open surgeries. This shift towards minimally invasive techniques is stimulating the market growth of lower limb arterial stents.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift towards drug-eluting stents (DES) as they have shown superior long-term outcomes compared to bare-metal stents. DES release drugs that help inhibit cell proliferation and minimize the risk of restenosis.</p><p>In conclusion, the lower limb arterial stents market is projected to experience substantial growth due to the increasing prevalence of PAD, advancements in stent technology, and the preference for minimally invasive procedures. The adoption of DES is also expected to rise, driven by their better long-term performance.</p></p>
