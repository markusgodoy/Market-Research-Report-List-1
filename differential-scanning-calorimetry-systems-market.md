<p><p>Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) systems are used to measure heat energy changes in materials as they undergo physical or chemical processes. There are several types of DSC systems available in the market. Heat Flux DSC measures the difference in heat flux between a sample and a reference material. Power Compensated DSC provides constant heating rates to accurately measure specific heat capacity. Modulated DSC measures both heat capacity and heat flow simultaneously. Hyper DSC offers high sensitivity and fast heating/cooling rates. Pressure DSC allows measurements under controlled pressure conditions.</p></p>
