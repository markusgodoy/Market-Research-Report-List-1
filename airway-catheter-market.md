<p><p>Airway catheters are medical devices used to maintain or secure an airway in patients who are unable to breathe spontaneously or effectively. They are commonly used in emergency situations, during surgeries, and in intensive care units. Airway catheters help in the clearing of secretions, blocking the entry of foreign materials into the lungs, and delivering medication directly to the respiratory system.</p><p>The global airway catheter market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.20% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this market growth. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases such as chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and asthma is driving the demand for airway catheters. Moreover, the rising geriatric population, which is more vulnerable to respiratory ailments, is also boosting market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology and product innovation in airway catheters are leading to the development of more efficient and safer devices. For instance, the introduction of video laryngoscopes and fiber-optic bronchoscopes has improved the success rate of intubation procedures.</p><p>Additionally, the COVID-19 pandemic has increased the demand for airway catheters due to the surge in hospitalizations and the need for respiratory support. <p><p>Airway catheters refer to medical devices used for administering oxygen or maintaining airway patency in patients who require respiratory support. The airway catheter market is divided into two types: disposable airway catheters and reusable airway catheters. Disposable airway catheters are single-use devices designed for one-time use and are disposed of after each use. Reusable airway catheters, on the other hand, are designed to be cleaned, disinfected, and reused multiple times. Both types of catheters serve the same purpose but differ in terms of single-use or multiple-use capabilities.</p></p>
