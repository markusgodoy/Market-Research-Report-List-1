<p><p>Oral films are thin, flat, and flexible strips made from polymers that are placed in the oral cavity for medication delivery. There are two main types of oral films: orodispersible film and oromucosal film. Orodispersible films dissolve rapidly in the mouth, delivering medication for conditions like allergies or pain relief. On the other hand, oromucosal films release drugs slowly over time, providing longer-lasting effects. Both types offer advantages such as ease of administration, convenience, and improved patient compliance in comparison to traditional dosage forms like tablets or capsules.</p></p>
