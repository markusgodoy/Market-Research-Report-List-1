<p><ul><li>Mechanical Lockset</li><li>Smart Lockset</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Locksets are classified into two main market types: mechanical locksets and smart locksets.</p><p>Mechanical locksets are traditional and rely on physical mechanisms for operation. They typically consist of a cylinder, latch, and key mechanism, providing basic security functions. Mechanical locksets are simpler and more affordable compared to smart locksets. They are commonly found in residential and commercial properties worldwide.</p><p>On the other hand, smart locksets incorporate advanced technology into the locking system. They offer keyless entry options and can be controlled remotely using smartphones or tablets. Smart locksets often provide additional features like temporary access codes, activity logs, and integration with home automation systems. While smart locksets are more expensive, they provide enhanced convenience and security for modern homes and businesses.</p></p>
