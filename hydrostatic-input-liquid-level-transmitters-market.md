<p><p>Hydrostatic input liquid level transmitters are used to measure the level of liquids in various applications. Rod level transmitters operate based on the principle of hydrostatic pressure, where a rod senses the pressure generated by the liquid column. Cable level transmitters, on the other hand, use a flexible cable that is immersed in the liquid and detects the pressure exerted. Both types have their advantages and are suitable for different scenarios depending on factors like the liquid's characteristics, application requirements, and installation constraints.</p></p>
