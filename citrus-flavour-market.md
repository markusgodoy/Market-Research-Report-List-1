<p><p>The citrus flavor market can be divided into two types: natural ingredients and artificial ingredients. Natural ingredients encompass flavors derived directly from citrus fruits, using methods like cold-pressing or steam distillation. These provide a fresh and authentic citrus taste to food and beverages. On the other hand, the artificial ingredients market includes flavors created synthetically in laboratories, using chemical compounds. While artificial flavors may mimic citrus taste, they lack the complexity and natural aroma found in their natural counterparts. The choice between natural and artificial ingredients depends on preference, cost, and desired flavor outcome.</p></p>
