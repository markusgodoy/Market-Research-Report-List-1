<p><p>Transformer rectifiers are devices used to convert alternating current (AC) to direct current (DC) for various industrial applications. There are three types of transformer rectifiers based on the cooling mechanism. Air cooled transformer rectifiers use air flow to dissipate heat, making them cost-effective and suitable for low-power applications. Oil cooled transformer rectifiers involve oil as a coolant, making them suitable for high-power applications due to their high cooling efficiency. Water cooled transformer rectifiers use water for cooling and are typically used in high-power applications where efficient cooling is required.</p></p>
