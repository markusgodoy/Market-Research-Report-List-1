<p><p>Sunflower seed oil is categorized into three main types based on their fatty acid composition. Linoleic sunflower seed oil contains a high percentage of omega-6 fatty acids, making it suitable for cooking and frying purposes. Mid-oleic sunflower seed oil has a balanced composition of oleic and linoleic acids, making it versatile for various culinary applications. High-oleic sunflower seed oil has a high content of oleic acid and offers improved stability and a longer shelf life, thus often used in the food industry for frying, baking, and other high-heat applications.</p></p>
