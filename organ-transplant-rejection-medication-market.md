<p><p>Organ transplant rejection medication, also known as immunosuppressants, are drugs that are prescribed to patients who have undergone an organ transplant surgery. Organ transplant rejection medication plays a vital role in improving the chances of successful organ transplantation and long-term survival.</p><p>The Organ Transplant Rejection Medication Market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>Organ transplant rejection medication market includes various types of drugs used to prevent the rejection of transplanted organs. Calcineurin inhibitors, such as cyclosporine and tacrolimus, work by suppressing the immune system. Antiproliferative agents, like azathioprine and mycophenolate mofetil, help control cell growth in the immune system. mTOR inhibitors, such as sirolimus and everolimus, prevent immune cells from multiplying. Antibodies, such as basiliximab and alemtuzumab, target specific immune cells. Steroids, like prednisone and methylprednisolone, reduce inflammation and suppress the immune response. These medications aim to maintain the functionality of transplanted organs.</p></p>
