<p><p>Remote clinical trials refer to studies conducted to assess the safety and effectiveness of drugs or medical interventions in participants who are geographically dispersed. There are three main types of remote clinical trials: interventional, observational, and expanded access. Interventional trials involve testing new treatments or interventions on participants to determine their effects. Observational trials collect data on participants' health outcomes without any intervention. Expanded access trials provide access to investigational treatments for patients with serious or life-threatening diseases who do not qualify for traditional clinical trials.</p></p>
