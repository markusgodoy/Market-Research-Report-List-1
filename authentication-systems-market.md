<p><p>Authentication Systems Market Types refers to different types of authentication methods used for securing access to a system or service. Two Factor Authentication (2FA) requires users to provide two types of identification, such as a password and a fingerprint. Multi Factor Authentication (MFA) involves the use of at least two or more authenticators, such as a password, a fingerprint, and a security question. Single Factor Authentication (SFA) is the most basic form of authentication, where users only provide one type of identification, usually a password. These market types offer varying levels of security and are used based on specific security requirements.</p></p>
