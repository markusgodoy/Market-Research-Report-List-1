<p><p>Zero-turn mowers are highly maneuverable lawn mowers that have a turning radius of zero degrees. These mowers are driven by two hydrostatic transmission-driven rear wheels, which allow them to rotate on the spot. The ability to make tight turns and navigate around obstacles quickly and efficiently makes zero-turn mowers popular among homeowners and professional landscapers.</p><p>The zero-turn mowers market has been experiencing steady growth in recent years. The increasing preference for easy-to-use and time-saving lawn mowers among homeowners is one of the primary factors driving market growth. Additionally, the rising demand for commercial landscaping services and the need for large-scale lawn maintenance in golf courses, sports fields, and parks are also contributing to the market's growth.</p><p>Technological advancements have also played a crucial role in shaping the market trends of zero-turn mowers. Manufacturers are increasingly focusing on introducing advanced features, such as improved steering controls, enhanced cutting systems, and ergonomic designs, to attract more customers. <p><p>Zero-turn mowers are available in two main market types: gas and electric. Gas-powered models are equipped with internal combustion engines that run on gasoline, offering greater power and cutting efficiency. They are ideal for large areas and rough terrain, where maximum performance is required. They are suitable for smaller yards and noise-sensitive environments, as they produce less vibration and emissions compared to gas models.</p></p>
