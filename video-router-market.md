<p><p>A video router, also known as a video switcher or video matrix switcher, is a fundamental component of video distribution systems. Additionally, the rise in live events and sports broadcasting has contributed to the increasing adoption of video routers.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the video router market is the shift towards IP-based video routing solutions. Traditional video routers typically use baseband technology, but with the increasing transition to IP networks, there is a growing demand for video routers that support IP protocols. IP-based video routers offer flexibility, scalability, and cost efficiency, making them highly attractive to end-users.</p><p>Furthermore, the integration of advanced features and technologies such as 4K/UHD (Ultra High Definition), HDR (High Dynamic Range), and virtualization capabilities is observed in the video router market.
