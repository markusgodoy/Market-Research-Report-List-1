<p><p>IV Cannulas, also known as intravenous cannulas or IV catheters, are medical devices used to administer medication or fluids directly into the bloodstream. They have a hollow, thin tube made of plastic or silicone, with a sharp needle at one end that is inserted into a vein. Once the needle is inside, it is retracted, leaving only the hollow tube in the vein, through which fluids or medication can be delivered.</p><p>The IV Cannulas Market has been witnessing significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue to do so in the coming years. The market growth can be attributed to several factors, including the rise in the prevalence of chronic diseases requiring intravenous therapy, the growing geriatric population, and the increasing number of surgical procedures. Additionally, technological advancements in IV cannulas, such as the development of safety IV cannulas to prevent needlestick injuries, are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>Another trend observed in the IV Cannulas Market is the increasing adoption of peripheral intravenous cannulas, which are commonly used in hospitals, clinics, and ambulatory surgery centers. Peripheral IV cannulas are easier to insert, have a lower risk of complications, and can be used for both short-term and long-term intravenous therapy.</p><p>Furthermore, the demand for IV cannulas is expected to rise due to the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. As hospitals and healthcare facilities continue to treat COVID-19 patients, the need for intravenous therapy to manage severe cases and administer medications such as remdesivir is increasing.</p><p>Overall, with the growing prevalence of chronic diseases, advancements in IV cannula technology, and the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic, the IV Cannulas Market is expected to witness significant growth at a CAGR of 10.10% during the forecast period.</p></p>

<p><p>IV Cannulas are medical devices used to administer fluids and medications into the veins. Peripheral IV Cannulas are commonly used for short-term treatments and are inserted in the arms or hands. Central Line IV Cannulas are used for long-term treatments and are inserted into a large vein near the heart. Midline IV Cannulas are intermediate-length catheters inserted in the upper arm, above the elbow. These different types of IV Cannulas serve specific purposes and are selected based on the duration and nature of the treatment required by patients.</p></p>
