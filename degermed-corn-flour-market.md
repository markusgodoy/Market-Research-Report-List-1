<p><p>Degermed corn flour is a type of flour that is made by removing the germ from corn kernels. The germ contains fat and oils, which can cause the flour to become rancid over time. By removing the germ, degermed corn flour has a longer shelf life and is more stable compared to regular corn flour.</p><p>The degermed corn flour market has been experiencing significant growth in recent years. This can be attributed to the increasing demand for gluten-free and non-GMO food products. Degermed corn flour is naturally gluten-free and can be used as a substitute for wheat flour in various recipes, making it a popular choice among individuals with gluten sensitivities or celiac disease.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising consumer awareness regarding the health benefits of whole grains has also contributed to the market growth. Degermed corn flour contains the endosperm of corn, which is rich in nutrients such as carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals. It is considered a healthier alternative to refined flour, leading to its increased adoption in various food applications.</p><p>In terms of market trends, the degermed corn flour market is witnessing a surge in product innovations. Manufacturers are focusing on developing value-added degermed corn flour products by incorporating different flavors, textures, and nutritional enhancements. This has expanded the application scope of degermed corn flour beyond traditional corn-based products, such as tortillas and chips, to include bakery products, snacks, and breakfast cereals.</p><p>The market is also witnessing a significant rise in the adoption of online retail channels, which allows manufacturers to reach a wider consumer base. Online platforms provide convenience, a wide range of product options, and competitive pricing, driving the market growth further.</p><p>Overall, with the growing demand for gluten-free and healthier food options, coupled with product innovations and the expansion of online retail channels, the degermed corn flour market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.8% during the forecast period.</p></p>
