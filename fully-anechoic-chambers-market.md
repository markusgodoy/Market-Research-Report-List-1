<p><p>Anechoic chambers are specialized rooms designed to minimize sound reflections, creating a space with virtually no echoes. There are three types of anechoic chambers: Standard Wedge, Perforated Metallic Wedge, and Sound Absorbing Wedge. </p><p>Standard Wedge chambers use foam or fiberglass wedges to absorb sound waves, preventing reflections. </p><p>Perforated Metallic Wedge chambers have metal wedges with perforated holes to allow sound waves to pass through and get absorbed by the absorptive material behind the wedges. </p><p>Sound Absorbing Wedge chambers use special materials that absorb sound waves directly, eliminating the need for additional wedges. These chambers are ideal for testing acoustic equipment.</p></p>
