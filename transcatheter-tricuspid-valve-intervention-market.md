<p><p>Transcatheter Tricuspid Valve Intervention (TTVI) is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat tricuspid valve disease. The tricuspid valve is located between the right atrium and right ventricle of the heart, and its main function is to prevent the backflow of blood from the ventricle to the atrium. However, various conditions such as tricuspid regurgitation and tricuspid stenosis can lead to the dysfunction of this valve, necessitating intervention.</p><p>TTVI involves the use of a catheter-based approach to repair or replace the tricuspid valve without the need for open-heart surgery. The procedure can be performed through several methods, including balloon valvuloplasty, edge-to-edge repair, and valve-in-valve or valve-in-ring implantation. TTVI offers several advantages over traditional surgical options, such as reduced risks, shorter hospital stays, and quicker recovery times for patients.</p><p>The Transcatheter Tricuspid Valve Intervention Market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing prevalence of tricuspid valve diseases, primarily driven by an aging population and the rising incidence of risk factors such as heart failure and atrial fibrillation, is a major factor contributing to market growth.
