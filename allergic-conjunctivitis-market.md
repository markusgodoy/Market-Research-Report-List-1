There are different types of allergic conjunctivitis: </p><p>1. Seasonal allergic conjunctivitis occurs during specific seasons, usually due to outdoor allergens like pollen.</p><p>2. Perennial allergic conjunctivitis is a year-round condition caused by indoor allergens like dust mites or pet dander.</p><p>3. Giant papillary conjunctivitis is characterized by the formation of large bumps on the inside of the eyelids and is often caused by contact lens wear.</p><p>4. Vernal keratoconjunctivitis is a rare condition that primarily affects children and is associated with a severe allergic response.</p><p>5. Atopic keratoconjunctivitis is a chronic condition that affects individuals with a history of atopic dermatitis or asthma.</p></p>
