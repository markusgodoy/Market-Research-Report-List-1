<p><p>Recording headphones can be categorized into two types: professional and amateur. Professional headphones are designed for professional audio engineers and musicians who require high-quality sound reproduction and accuracy in the recording and mixing process. These headphones typically have a flat frequency response, closed-back designs for noise isolation, and durable construction. On the other hand, amateur headphones are more suited for casual listeners and beginners in audio recording, emphasizing comfort and affordability over high-end audio reproduction. They often have a more consumer-friendly sound signature and may lack specific features found in professional models.</p></p>
