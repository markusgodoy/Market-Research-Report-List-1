<p><p>Cervical orthoses, or neck braces, are devices used to provide support and stability to the neck. Dynamic orthotics are cervical orthoses that allow controlled movement of the neck while still providing support for injured or weak cervical regions. These braces use technologies such as springs or hinges to enable controlled motion. On the other hand, static orthotics restrict the movement of the neck entirely, immobilizing it to promote healing and prevent further damage. These devices are typically rigid in nature and are commonly used in cases of severe neck injuries or post-surgery recovery.</p></p>
