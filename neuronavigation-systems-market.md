<p><p>Neuronavigation systems are used in neurosurgery to assist surgeons in accurately accessing and navigating the brain. Optical systems utilize cameras and infrared light to track surgical instruments in real-time. They provide precise localization but may be obstructed by anatomical structures. On the other hand, electromagnetic systems use electromagnetic fields to track the instruments, offering better accuracy and less interference from obstructions. Both systems aid in improving the safety and success of neurosurgical procedures by providing real-time guidance and visualization.</p></p>
