<p><strong>The Lung Cancer Diagnostics Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Immunoassays</li><li>Flow Cytometry</li><li>Rapid Tests</li><li>Molecular Assays</li><li>Tissue Arrays</li><li>Circulating Tumor Cells</li><li>Pharmacodiagnostics</li><li>Biomarkers</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The lung cancer diagnostics market includes various types of tests used to detect and diagnose lung cancer. Immunoassays are laboratory-based tests that measure the levels of certain substances in the blood or other fluids to identify cancer markers. Flow Cytometry is a method to identify and analyze cancer cells based on their physical and chemical properties. Rapid tests provide quick results by detecting specific cancer markers. Molecular assays detect genetic mutations in lung cancer cells. Tissue arrays analyze multiple tissue samples on a single slide. Circulating Tumor Cells are blood tests that detect cancer cells in the bloodstream. Pharmacodiagnostics involve identifying drugs that are effective for specific patients. Biomarkers are substances or genetic characteristics that indicate the presence of cancer. Other types of tests may include imaging techniques or clinical examinations.</p></p>
