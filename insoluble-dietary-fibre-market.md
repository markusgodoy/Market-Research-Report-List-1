<p><p>The Insoluble Dietary Fiber market can be divided into various types, including cellulose, hemicellulose, chitin and chitosan, lignin, bran, resistant starch, and others. Cellulose is found in plant cell walls and is the most abundant type of dietary fiber. Hemicellulose also comes from plant cells and provides structural support. Chitin and chitosan are derived from shellfish and have numerous applications in various industries. Lignin is found in woody plants and provides rigidity. Bran is rich in fiber and is commonly used as a cereal ingredient. Resistant starch is a type of starch that resists digestion in the small intestine. Other types of insoluble dietary fibers include gums, pectins, and psyllium.</p></p>
