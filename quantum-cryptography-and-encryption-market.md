<p><p>Quantum cryptography and encryption are advanced security techniques that harness the principles of quantum mechanics to protect sensitive data from unauthorized access or decryption. Traditional encryption methods rely on mathematical algorithms that can be potentially broken by future advancements in computing power, whereas quantum cryptography ensures unbreakable encryption by utilizing the properties of quantum mechanics.</p><p>Quantum cryptography offers a secure method of key distribution, where cryptographic keys are exchanged between two parties. This process involves encoding each bit of the key onto individual photons or quantum bits (qubits), which are then transmitted over a secure channel. Any interception or tampering with the qubits will disturb their quantum state, alerting the parties to the presence of an eavesdropper.</p><p>The market for quantum cryptography and encryption is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. The increasing complexity and frequency of cyber-attacks, as well as the growing adoption of the internet of things (IoT) and cloud-based services, are driving the demand for robust security solutions.
