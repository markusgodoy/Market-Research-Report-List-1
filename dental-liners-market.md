<p><p>Dental liners are materials used in dentistry to protect the tooth structure and provide a barrier between the tooth and restorative materials. Two common types of dental liners are glass ionomer and calcium hydroxide. Glass ionomer liners are made of a mixture of glass and acrylic acids that form a chemical bond with the tooth, releasing fluoride and promoting tooth remineralization. Calcium hydroxide liners contain calcium hydroxide, which helps in pulp protection and dentin bridge formation. Both types serve as a protective layer and aid in the longevity of dental restorations.</p></p>
