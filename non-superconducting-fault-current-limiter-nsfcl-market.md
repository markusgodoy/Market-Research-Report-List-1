<p><p>The non-superconducting fault current limiter (NSFCL) market consists of two main types: saturable core and solid state. In the saturable core market, the limiter utilizes a magnetic core material that saturates when a fault current exceeds a certain level, thereby limiting the current flow. On the other hand, the solid state market involves the use of semiconductor devices to control and regulate the fault current. Both types of NSFCL offer effective solutions to mitigate fault currents, enhancing the reliability and performance of electrical power systems.</p></p>
