<p><p>Fixed-height veterinary examination tables are available in two main types: manual and non-adjustable. Manual tables require human intervention to adjust their height, allowing veterinarians to find a comfortable working position. On the other hand, non-adjustable tables have a fixed height that cannot be changed. These tables are designed to cater to specific veterinary procedures and offer stability during examinations. While manual tables provide flexibility, non-adjustable tables are preferred when a consistent height is sufficient for routine examinations. Ultimately, the choice between these types depends on the specific needs and preferences of veterinarians and their clinics.</p></p>
