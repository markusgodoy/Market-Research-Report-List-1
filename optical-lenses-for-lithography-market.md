<p><p>Optical lenses for lithography are crucial components used in semiconductor manufacturing processes. Lenses for DUV (deep ultraviolet) lithography are designed to operate in the UV range, typically around 248 nm wavelength. They enable precise patterning of smaller features on the semiconductor wafer. On the other hand, lenses for EUV (extreme ultraviolet) lithography are designed to handle shorter wavelengths of around 13.5 nm. EUV lenses enable even smaller feature sizes, improving the resolution and accuracy for next-generation semiconductor devices. Both types of lenses play a pivotal role in the advancement of lithography technology.</p></p>

<p><p>Optical lenses are crucial components in lithography, used for printing patterns onto semiconductor wafers during the fabrication process. In contact printing lithography, the lens is in direct contact with the wafer, providing high resolution but limited scale. Proximity printing lithography involves a small gap between the lens and the wafer, allowing larger scale but lower resolution. Projection printing lithography employs a lens to project patterns onto the wafer, balancing resolution and scale. Other applications include deep ultraviolet lithography, double patterning lithography, and electron-beam lithography, each providing unique advantages for specific semiconductor manufacturing processes.</p></p>
