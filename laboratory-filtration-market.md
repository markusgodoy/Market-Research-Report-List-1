<p><p>The laboratory filtration market consists of several types of filtration techniques: ultrafiltration, microfiltration, nanofiltration, and reverse osmosis. Ultrafiltration employs a membrane with small pores to separate particles, solutes, and macromolecules from a liquid sample. Microfiltration also employs a membrane, but with larger pores, to remove particles and microorganisms. Nanofiltration utilizes a membrane with even smaller pores to remove multivalent ions, smaller molecules, and some microorganisms. Reverse osmosis uses a highly selective membrane to separate dissolved solutes from water. These filtration techniques enable precise purification and separation in laboratory settings.</p></p>
