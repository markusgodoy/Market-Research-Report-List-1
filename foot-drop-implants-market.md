<p><p>Foot drop implants refer to medical devices used to treat foot drop, a condition characterized by weakness or paralysis of the muscles that control foot movement. There are two main types of foot drop implants: internal fixation devices and functional electric stimulators. Internal fixation devices are surgically implanted to provide mechanical support and stabilize the foot. On the other hand, functional electric stimulators use electrical current to stimulate the nerves or muscles, helping to restore foot movement. These implants are designed to improve mobility and quality of life for individuals with foot drop.</p></p>
